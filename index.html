Course Description

Interaction design shapes how people experience the digital world, from the interfaces we tap to the systems we trust. This studio course introduces the principles, processes, and mindsets of designing meaningful interactions. Through a mix of lectures, hands-on creation, and group critique, students will learn to prototype ideas, explore creative technologies, and design with empathy and purpose. The course emphasizes experimentation, reflection, and collaboration to cultivate both technical fluency and a designer‚Äôs sensibility.

Learning Objectives

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

  1. Apply core principles of interaction design to create thoughtful, usable interfaces.
  2. Prototype and test interactive ideas through iterative, hands-on methods.
  3. Communicate design concepts visually and verbally in critiques and presentations.
  4. Reflect on how design choices shape user experience, emotion, and inclusion.
